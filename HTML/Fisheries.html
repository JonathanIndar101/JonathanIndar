Fisheries

In Trinidad and Tobago, the fish market is one of the main sources of food and fishermen income. The Fisheries Division in Trinidad and Tobago have been established to create and promote the management, sustainable development, and regulation of fisheries. The Fisheries Sector plays a crucial role in ensuring that fish stocks are captured safely and efficiently and ensuring the economic benefits while protecting our marine life.

Key Areas of the Fisheries Division

  • Stock Assessments
  • Catch Limits
  • Resource Management

Challenges Faced by the Fisheries Divisions

Overfishing and Resource Depletion: With the demand of seafood products, alot of sea creatures are being harvested more rapidily than the fishess population.

Pollution and Habitat Destruction: Pollution such as plastic waste, oil spills from errupted piplines in the sea or ships are currently affecting our sea creatures and are reducing our aqua habitat species.

Policies Made By the Ministry

Some of our policies that the ministry have implemented are:

  • Quota Systems: Limits for the amount of fish that can be caught.
  • Fishing Regulations Regulate the times for fishing to ensure protection and breeding opportunities for sea creatures.
